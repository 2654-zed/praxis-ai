Assessment Methodology

How Praxis evaluates AI tools for enterprise procurement

Overview

Praxis provides automated vendor intelligence for AI tool procurement. Every tool in our database is assessed across two proprietary indices — Transparency and Strategic Flexibility — alongside risk indicators, dependency profiling, and data exposure mapping.

Our methodology is designed to surface the information that procurement teams, CTOs, and technology leaders need but vendor marketing rarely provides.

Assessment Framework

For every tool evaluated, Praxis generates a Vendor Intelligence Report covering five dimensions:

  • Ownership & Funding — Corporate structure, investor alignment, revenue incentives
  • Data Exposure Map — What organizational data is processed, stored, and potentially leveraged
  • Dependency Risk — Lock-in level, switching costs, exit strategy viability
  • Compliance Posture — Certifications, audit history, regulatory alignment
  • Strategic Flexibility — Portability, self-hosting options, multi-vendor compatibility

Transparency Score

What It Measures

The Transparency Score (0–100) evaluates how clearly a vendor discloses its practices around data handling, revenue model, ownership, and compliance. Higher scores indicate greater visibility for procurement decision-making.

Scoring factors:

  • Open Source (+25) — Code-level auditability provides maximum transparency
  • SOC2 Attestation (+8) — Independent third-party security audit
  • GDPR Compliance (+7) — European data protection standards adherence
  • HIPAA Compliance (+10) — Healthcare-grade data protection
  • Revenue Model Clarity (+5/−5) — Direct subscription vs. unclear monetization
  • Data Practice Documentation (+5/−5) — Specific vs. vague data handling disclosure
  • Confirmed Ownership (+5/−5) — Known vendor vs. unverified entity

Strategic Flexibility Score

What It Measures

The Flexibility Score (0–100) assesses how much operational independence a tool provides. It evaluates portability, lock-in risk, switching costs, and whether the tool enhances organizational capability or creates vendor dependency.

Scoring factors:

  • Open Source (+20) — Self-hosting, forking, and migration options available
  • Lock-in Level (−5 to −20) — Based on vendor dependency assessment
  • Data Portability (+10/−10) — Export capabilities and format standardization
  • Integration Surface (+5/−3) — Multi-vendor compatibility (balanced against ecosystem lock-in)
  • Evaluation Access (+5/−3) — Free tier availability for low-risk assessment

Risk Indicators

Praxis automatically flags areas where vendor positioning may not fully align with observable practices. These aren't disqualifiers — they're items for your procurement checklist:

  • Free tier without open source — Revenue model may involve data monetization
  • AI positioning with high lock-in — Innovation claims should be weighed against dependency cost
  • Custom enterprise pricing — Request detailed pricing schedules and limit clauses
  • Compliance as differentiator — Verify certifications are current and scope-appropriate
  • Disproportionate data exposure — Ensure vendor data access is proportionate to value

Vendor Intelligence Database

Praxis maintains curated intelligence profiles for 25+ major AI vendors including ownership structure, revenue models, data handling documentation, lock-in assessments, and portability analysis. For tools outside our curated database, we apply heuristic analysis based on observable attributes: pricing structure, compliance claims, category, and integration surface.

All intelligence is derived from public filings, published documentation, and published policies. No vendor has paid for assessment or placement.
